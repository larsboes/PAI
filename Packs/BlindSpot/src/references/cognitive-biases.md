# Cognitive Biases That Create Blind Spots

Common biases that make blind spots invisible. Use as a checklist when the five lenses feel "clean" but something still feels off.

## Planning & Estimation

| Bias | What It Does | How to Counter |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Planning Fallacy** | Underestimate time/cost, overestimate capability | Ask: "How long did similar things actually take?" |
| **Optimism Bias** | Believe bad outcomes won't happen to us | Ask: "What would a pessimist say?" |
| **Anchoring** | First number/idea dominates all subsequent thinking | Ask: "If we started from scratch, would we arrive here?" |

## Scope & Completeness

| Bias | What It Does | How to Counter |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Survivorship Bias** | Only see successes, miss the silent failures | Ask: "What similar attempts failed and why?" |
| **Availability Heuristic** | Overweight recent/vivid examples | Ask: "What's the base rate for this kind of thing?" |
| **WYSIATI** (What You See Is All There Is) | Treat available info as complete | Ask: "What data don't we have?" |

## Social & Stakeholder

| Bias | What It Does | How to Counter |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Curse of Knowledge** | Can't unsee what you know — miss novice perspective | Ask: "How would someone new to this experience it?" |
| **False Consensus** | Assume others think like us | Ask: "Who would disagree and why?" |
| **Authority Bias** | Accept decisions from authority without scrutiny | Ask: "If a junior proposed this, would we accept it?" |

## Technical & Design

| Bias | What It Does | How to Counter |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Sunk Cost** | Continue investing because we already invested | Ask: "If we started today, would we choose this?" |
| **Complexity Bias** | Prefer complex solutions over simple ones | Ask: "What's the simplest thing that could work?" |
| **Status Quo Bias** | Prefer current state even when change is better | Ask: "If we had neither, which would we build?" |
| **Ikea Effect** | Overvalue what we built ourselves | Ask: "Would we adopt this if someone else built it?" |

## When to Consult This List

- When all five lenses return "nothing significant" — that itself is suspicious
- When a decision was made very quickly without friction
- When everyone agreed too easily
- When the solution looks elegant — elegance hides complexity
