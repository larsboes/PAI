# Brainstorm Reference: Facilitation Patterns

## Asking Good Questions

Questions are the brainstorm's engine. The right question opens new territory.

### Opening Questions (start of session)
- "What does good look like here? What's the dream outcome?"
- "What's the constraint you're most frustrated by?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would you change?"
- "Who else has solved something like this? (different field)"
- "What would you do if failure wasn't possible?"

### Deepening Questions (during explore)
- "What would need to be true for this to work?"
- "What's the simplest version of this that delivers value?"
- "What are you assuming that might not be true?"
- "If this succeeds perfectly, what does month 3 look like?"
- "What would make you change your mind?"

### Challenging Questions (when too easy)
- "You're gravitating toward [X]. What scares you about [Y]?"
- "Everyone does [conventional approach]. What's wrong with it that people don't talk about?"
- "Is this solving the problem or working around it?"
- "What would a competitor/critic say about this approach?"
- "What's the laziest version of this that might still work?"

### Convergence Questions (when deciding)
- "If you had to pick today, which one? What made you hesitate?"
- "What's the smallest experiment that would tell you if [idea] works?"
- "In 6 months, which choice would you regret more — trying this and failing, or not trying?"
- "What's the reversibility of each option? How costly is 'wrong'?"

## Recognizing Phase Transitions

### User is ready to move from DIVERGE → EXPLORE when:
- They say "I like [X]" or "Tell me more about [Y]"
- They ask "How would that work?" or "Is that feasible?"
- They react with enthusiasm to specific ideas
- They start combining ideas themselves

### User is ready to move from EXPLORE → CONVERGE when:
- They say "Ok, so which one?" or "What should I do?"
- They've expressed clear preferences with consistent criteria
- New exploration rounds aren't producing new insights
- They start getting impatient or repeating themselves

### User should go BACK to DIVERGE when:
- All explored ideas have problems and nothing satisfies
- The criteria shifted (means the frame changed)
- They say "none of these feel right"
- A new constraint or opportunity emerged

## Handling Common Session Dynamics

### "I already know what I want, just validate it"
Don't just validate. Spend 2 minutes ensuring it's been stress-tested:
> "That sounds solid. Let me play devil's advocate for 30 seconds to make sure we're not missing something. [One good challenge]. Does that change anything?"

### "I have no idea where to start"
Frame the problem first, then diverge broadly:
> "Let's start with what we know. What's the problem, who's it for, and what constraints are non-negotiable? Once we have that, I'll generate a range of directions."

### "Too many options, I'm overwhelmed"
Force-rank immediately:
> "Let me simplify. Based on what you've said matters most ([criteria]), here are the top 3 in order. [Ranked list]. React to that ordering."

### "Can you just decide for me?"
Give a direct opinion, but make reasoning transparent:
> "If this were my decision, I'd go with [X]. Here's why: [2-3 sentences]. But you know [context I don't have] better than me. Does that reasoning apply to your situation?"

### "What if we just don't do anything?"
Valid option. Explore it:
> "Let's take 'do nothing' seriously. What happens in 3 months if we don't act? Is that acceptable? Sometimes the right move is to wait for more information."

## Integration Cheat Sheet

| Moment | Tool |
|--------|------|
| Need more diverse ideas | **BeCreative** (verbalized sampling, 5 internal options) |
| Need to evaluate an idea rigorously | **RedTeam** (steelman + counter-argument) |
| Need multiple expert perspectives | **Council** or `/swarm quick "..."` |
| Need to check if idea is technically feasible | `code_search` + `web_search` |
| Assumptions feel wrong | **FirstPrinciples** (challenge constraints) |
| Need to test an idea empirically | **Science** (design experiment) |
| Idea involves a hard technical problem | **DeepDebug** (investigate feasibility blockers) |
| Important life/career decision | `/swarm begin --with career-strategist` |
